\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Abstract}
\begin{abstract}
\begin{large}
The present report focuses on the analysis of modern Command-and-Control (C2) frameworks in the context of Red Team operations. C2 infrastructures represent a core component of realistic attack simulations, as they enable the centralized control of compromised systems and the execution of post-exploitation activities. \\
The work provides a technical examination of contemporary C2 frameworks, with a particular focus on the Sliver framework. In addition, established solutions such as Metasploit and Cobalt Strike are comparatively assessed to identify architectural differences, operational characteristics, and typical use cases. The results contribute to evaluating the suitability of modern C2 frameworks for penetration testing as a service offering within the Service Central Cyber-Security (SZC) department and provide a foundation for further investigations in the area of payload and communication obfuscation.
\end{large}
\end{abstract}
